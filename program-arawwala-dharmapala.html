Sparking Creativity and Innovation


On June 14, 2024, Arawwala Dharmapala Vidyalaya hosted the second workshop of the Nenasa STEM Education Workshop Series, bringing together 53 eager participants, including 28 girls and 25 boys, along with 3 dedicated teachers. This event aimed to foster a deeper understanding of STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) among students, equipping them with essential skills for the future.

The workshop commenced with an engaging introductory speech delivered by a student, who provided a brief overview of the day's schedule and activities. This student-led opening set a collaborative tone for the event, encouraging everyone to actively participate. Following the introduction, the Vice Principal welcomed all attendees and emphasized the importance of participation and engagement in the learning process. Her encouragement resonated with the students, reinforcing the idea that active involvement is key to maximizing the benefits of such workshops.

Chamodi Hansika, the Chairperson of Nenasa, then took the stage to share her insights with the students. Her inspiring words motivated the students to pursue their passions and explore the diverse opportunities available in various fields. Chamodi's emphasis on following one's interests ignited enthusiasm among the students, encouraging them to think beyond conventional career paths.

The workshop was led by Ms. Praveena, who guided participants through a series of engaging activities designed to enhance their understanding of STEM concepts. The hands-on approach allowed students to apply their knowledge practically, fostering creativity and critical thinking skills.

A significant part of the workshop focused on initiating an Inventors Club, aimed at encouraging students to collaborate on innovative projects and explore their creative potential. This initiative is expected to cultivate a community of young inventors who can share ideas and work together on exciting STEM challenges.

The event concluded with a heartfelt vote of thanks delivered by a student, who expressed gratitude to all contributors and highlighted the significance of such initiatives in advancing STEM education in Sri Lanka.

The positive feedback from participants reflected the event's profound impact, reinforcing our commitment to fostering a culture of continuous learning and innovation, not only enriching students' knowledge but also strengthening community ties, paving the way for brighter futures in STEM and beyond.
