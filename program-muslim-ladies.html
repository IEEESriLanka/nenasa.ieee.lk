Innovation and Robotics Workshop


On August 23rd, a special robotics and innovation program took place at Fathima Muslim Ladies College. The main purpose was to inspire students, spark creativity, and give them a chance to experience new technology through hands-on learning. The event was organized with the support of Nenansa members including Chamodi, Disara, Hashini, Abhishek and Sewmi, together with student branch volunteers from Uva Wellassa University who guided and supported the participants throughout the sessions.

A total of 58 students and 2 teachers took part in the program. The event began with a warm welcome from the students, creating an energetic and friendly atmosphere. The first session focused on innovation and robotics, encouraging students to think creatively about real-world problems and how technology can help solve them.

The practical sessions were the most engaging part of the day. Students began by building a robotic arm, learning about design, mechanics and basic programming. The next session expanded on robotics applications, giving everyone the chance to explore more complex ideas through teamwork and experimentation.

At the end of the program, the organizers donated Arduino kits to the school. This thoughtful gesture allowed students to continue exploring robotics beyond the event. The day was both educational and inspiring, leaving students motivated to pursue innovation and technology with confidence.
