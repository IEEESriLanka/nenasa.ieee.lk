Innovation and Robotics Workshop


On August 23rd, 2024, at 8:30 AM, we had an amazing robotics and innovation program at Fathima Muslim Ladies College. The main goal was to inspire students, spark creativity, and give everyone a chance to actually try out new technology instead of just hearing about it. The event had a really nice mix of people—Nenansa members Chamodi, Disara, Hashini, Abhishek and Sewmi were there, along with some super supportive student branch volunteers from Uva Wellassa University who shared their knowledge and guided us throughout the day.

The turnout was great, with 58 students and 2 teachers joining in. Things kicked off with a warm welcome from the students, which made the atmosphere feel really positive and friendly. Then we moved into an interactive session on innovation and robotics. It wasn’t just a boring lecture—it actually made us think differently about how technology can solve real problems and how creative ideas can turn into real projects.

After that, we jumped into the best part: the robotics practical sessions. The first activity was about building a robotic arm, which was both fun and challenging. We got to learn about design, mechanics, and even a bit of programming. The second session took things further, letting us explore more robotics applications. It was hands-on, engaging, and pushed us to work together as a team while experimenting with new ideas.

At the end of the day, there was a really thoughtful moment—the organizers donated Arduino kits to the school. This was such a cool gesture because it means students can keep learning and practicing even after the event is over. It wasn’t just about the one-day program; it felt like an investment in the future creativity of the students at Fathima Muslim Ladies College.
