STEM Camp - 2025


Overview

This initiative outlines a three-day STEM Camp designed to ignite curiosity and innovation in students from underserved communities across Sri Lanka. Targeting 150 students and 20 teachers from all 9 provinces, this initiative is grounded in experiential learning, inclusivity, and long-term impact. Hosted at the Open University of Sri Lanka (OUSL), Colombo, from November 8 to 10, the camp is tailored for Grade 9 & 10 students from schools with limited lab access for Science and IT. It integrates engaging DIY STEM activities, mentorship, innovation challenges, and a train-the-trainer component to empower both students and educators. The initiative reflects a strong commitment to advancing national STEM goals, encouraging equitable access to learning resources, and nurturing a culture of grassroots innovation.


Target Audience

- 150 students (Grades 9–10) from all 9 provinces of Sri Lanka
- 20 science/technology teachers selected from the same schools


Date and Venue

- Dates:November 8, 9 and 10
- Venue: TheOpen University of Sri Lanka (OUSL), Colombo


Outcomes to gain

- Increased awareness of STEM career and education pathways
- Confidence in using local/low-cost resources for science
- Basic understanding of sustainable innovation concepts
- Peer learning and team collaboration through challenges
- Teachers trained with practical take-home lesson plans
- Formation of science clubs for continuity in schools
